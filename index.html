Conveyancing Costs Explained

Conveyancing fees vary depending on location, property type, and service provider. In Victoria and Queensland, professional fees typically range from affordable fixed rates, ensuring transparency and no surprise bills.

Understanding the breakdown helps you budget accurately and avoid hidden charges.

Factors That Influence Conveyancing Fees

  • Property value and location (metro vs regional)
  • Transaction type (purchase, sale, or transfer)
  • Complexity (off-the-plan, commercial, or subdivisions)
  • Whether it's a buyer or seller instruction
  • State-specific requirements and searches
  • Provider's experience and service level

Typical Professional Conveyancing Fees

Many reputable conveyancers offer fixed-fee pricing. Approximate ranges (as of 2025):

Disbursements and Other Costs

Beyond professional fees, expect:

  • Search fees (title, council, water, etc.): $200–$500
  • PEXA settlement fees: $100–$200
  • Stamp duty (government tax, varies by property value and buyer status)
  • Adjustment calculations for rates and taxes

Transparent providers include most disbursements in their quote.

Choosing Value Over the Cheapest Price

While cost matters, the lowest quote isn't always best. Prioritise conveyancers who offer:

  • Fixed fees with no hidden extras
  • Licensing in both VIC and QLD if needed
  • Excellent communication and proactive service
  • Strong client reviews and proven experience
